Popular Forts in Raigad

The Raigad Fort

The fort is known for being the pride of the Marathas and is a symbol of bravery, therefore it is more than just being a tourist spot. Learn about the grand vision of Hindavi Swarajya as cherished by Chhatrapati Shivaji along with the brave history of Marathas when you visit the fort.

The Murud Jangira

Murud-Janjira is the local name of a famous fort and tourist spot situated on an island just off the coastal town of Murud, in the Raigad district of Maharashtra, India.

Karnala fort

Karnala Fort (also called Funnel Hill ) is a hill fort in Raigad district, Maharashtra, India, about 10 km from Panvel city. Currently it is a protected place lying within the Karnala Bird Sanctuary.

Revdanda Fort

Dating back to the 15th-century, this is one of the few beach-facing forts in Raigad district which was built by the Portuguese to protect their sea shipments. Initially known as Santa Maria de Castelo.

Kothaligad Fort

Also known as Peth Fort, the Kothaligad Fort is nestled amidst the charming surroundings of the Bhimashankar Wildlife Sanctuary. Amongst the most strategic forts in Raigad district, it used to serve as a garrison and a Watchtower during maratha time.

Sudhagad Fort

Also known as Bhorapgad, this is a small fort dating back to the 2nd-century BC that was captured by Shivaji from the Bahamanis in the 16th-century. After the annexation of the princely states, the fort had no ruler.

Sagargad Fort

Located in Alibaug, this hill fort is located amidst lush and thickly forested expanses offering beautiful sea views. Before being finally handed over to the British, the fort changed hands between rulers like Shivaji, Bhivaji Gujar etc.

Korlai Fort

One of the few island forts in Raigad district, built by the Portuguese to fortify the creek of Revdanda in the 15th-century, lies in a sleepy town in the Konkan range.

Kolaba Fort

Also known as the Alibag Fort, this is one of the most visited forts in Raigad district. Dating back to 300 years, this used to be a naval station for the army of Shivaji. You can see still-existing cannons, huge walls covered in animal carvings.

Manikgad Fort

This hilltop 18th-century fort located in the Manikgad village is one of the most visited forts in Raigad district. It was built by the Maratha armies to guard their trade routes and was ceded to the British later on.

Prabalgad Fort

Perching precariously on a boulder amidst the Sahyadri Mountain ranges at a height of around 2300 ft. above sea level, Prabalgad Fort is one of the most uniquely situated forts in the country.

Vikatgad Peb Fort

Vikatgad Peb Fort is located on the Matheran Hill Range, about 2,100 feet above sea level. It is a popular trekking spot for those seeking an adventurous trail. It is continuous to other forts of the Matheran Hill Range along with chanderi fort.
