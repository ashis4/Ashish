Kulaba Fort

A Seaside Marvel from Shivaji’s Era

Overview

Kulaba Fort is a magnificent sea fort located just off the Alibaug coast. Built during the reign of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, this fort was used as a naval base to safeguard the Maratha Empire. It becomes accessible by foot during low tide, while boats are required during high tide.

What To Expect?

The fort is known for its high walls, large cannons, and a beautifully preserved Siddhivinayak temple. Walking along the walls, you can enjoy panoramic views of the sea and Alibaug shoreline. The fort also houses freshwater wells, which is unique for a sea fort. The journey to the fort itself is an adventure, as visitors walk through the sea bed during low tide. Don’t miss exploring the historic artifacts and ruins scattered throughout the fort.

Tips

  • Check tide timings before planning your visit.
  • Wear waterproof footwear for walking through wet sand or shallow water.
  • Carry a flashlight to explore dark corners of the fort.
  • Avoid visiting during the monsoon due to high tides.
  • Combine your visit with a beach picnic.
