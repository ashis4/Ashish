Overview

Nestled in the Alibaug region, Sagargad Fort is a blend of history, nature, and adventure. It offers stunning views of the Arabian Sea, surrounded by waterfalls and lush greenery. Historically, the fort was used to monitor coastal activities and played a vital role during the Maratha reign.

What To Expect?

The trek begins with a scenic trail passing through dense forests, small streams, and ancient stone pathways. On reaching the top, you'll find the Siddheshwar temple, a small water tank, and the ruins of the fort's bastions. The Vanartok Pinnacle, a unique rock formation, is a key attraction for climbers. The fort provides a panoramic view of the Alibaug coastline and nearby villages. During the monsoon, the surrounding waterfalls and misty ambiance add to its charm. Be prepared for a slightly steep climb, especially near the pinnacle.

Tips

  • Wear sturdy shoes for the rocky terrain.
  • Avoid visiting during heavy monsoon as trails become slippery.
  • Carry a raincoat if visiting in rainy weather.
  • Visit the nearby Siddheshwar Temple for a peaceful break.
  • Plan a weekend trip to include nearby forts or beaches.
