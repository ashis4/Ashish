Manikgad Fort

An ancient Fortress Amidist Nature

Overview

Manikgad Fort, also known as Gadchandur Fort, is an ancient hill fort built by the Bhoj dynasty. Located near Panvel, this fort offers an offbeat trekking experience amidst lush greenery and tranquil surroundings. Once a center of trade and defense, the fort now stands as a testament to its historical significance.

What To Expect?

The trek to Manikgad Fort is moderately challenging, starting with dense forests and gradually ascending to rocky trails. Along the way, you’ll encounter remnants of ancient fortifications, stone pathways, and water reservoirs. At the summit, the ruins of the Shiva temple and the fort’s bastions provide a glimpse into its glorious past. The fort offers stunning views of the Karnala and Prabalgad ranges, making it a perfect spot for photography and relaxation. With its serene atmosphere and relatively less crowded trails, it is ideal for those seeking solitude in nature.

Tips

  • Start early to complete the trek before noon.
  • Carry enough water as there are no facilities at the top.
  • Be prepared for uneven and steep patches on the trail.
  • Wear a hat and sunscreen to protect from direct sunlight.
  • Avoid trekking alone; go with a group or guide.
