Prabalgad Fort

A Scenic Escape with Historical Significance

Overview

Prabalgad Fort, also known as Muranjan Fort, is a historic marvel perched atop the Western Ghats near Panvel. Overlooking the Matheran hill station, it is known for its strategic location and mesmerizing views of the Sahyadri ranges. The fort was used as a military base during the Bahamani Sultanate and later during the Maratha Empire.

What To Expect?

The trek to Prabalgad Fort is moderately challenging, featuring steep ascents through dense forests and rocky terrain. Once you reach the plateau, the vast expanse and panoramic views of Kalavantin Durg, Matheran, and the surrounding valleys will leave you spellbound. The fort features ruins of stone structures, walls, and water tanks. The trail is dotted with diverse flora and fauna, making it a delight for nature lovers. Watching the sunrise or sunset from the fort is an unforgettable experience.

Tips

  • Wear proper trekking shoes for the rocky trail.
  • Bring a camera to capture the breathtaking landscapes.
  • Avoid visiting during monsoon due to slippery paths.
  • Stay hydrated and carry energy bars for the trek.
  • Combine the trek with Kalavantin Durg for a complete adventure.
