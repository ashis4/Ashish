Raigad Fort

A Spiritual and Adventurous Trek

Overview

Vikatgad Peb Fort, located near Neral, is a trekker’s paradise known for its challenging climb and spectacular views. The fort is named "Peb" due to the presence of the Goddess Pebi shrine at the top. It is believed to have been used as a watchtower during the reign of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj.

What To Expect?

The trek to Vikatgad Peb Fort offers a mix of dense forests, waterfalls, ladders for steep climbs, and rocky terrain. Along the way, you’ll encounter a unique cave, a Buddhist Vihara, and the ruins of fortifications. The summit provides stunning views of the Matheran range and the surrounding countryside. The peaceful atmosphere and natural beauty make it a favorite among trekkers and nature enthusiasts. The fort also offers an opportunity for night treks, allowing you to witness the magical sunrise from the top.

Tips

  • Bring gloves for climbing ladders and navigating rocky sections.
  • Plan a group trek for safety, especially for night treks.
  • Carry a flashlight or headlamp for dark sections.
  • Be mindful of monkeys near the base village; secure your belongings.
  • Wear comfortable clothing and pack light for an enjoyable trek.
