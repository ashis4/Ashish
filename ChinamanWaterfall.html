Chinaman’s Waterfall

The Exotic Cascade of Mahabaleshwar

Overview

Chinaman’s Waterfall is another gem of Mahabaleshwar, located close to the town’s main attractions. The waterfall derives its name from the Chinese prisoners who were believed to have maintained the gardens nearby. Falling from a height of 500 feet, it offers a mesmerizing sight, especially during the monsoon season.

What To Expect?

The waterfall is surrounded by lush greenery, creating a picturesque backdrop for photography. The gushing water forms a pool at the base, though access to the base may be restricted during heavy rains for safety. The cascading water, misty surroundings, and cool breeze make it an ideal place for relaxation.

Tips

  • Visit early in the day to avoid crowds.
  • Wear comfortable clothing and waterproof shoes.
  • Avoid visiting during heavy rainfall as the area may be slippery.
  • Take binoculars for a closer view from the observation deck.
  • Combine the visit with Wilson Point or Elphinstone Point nearby.
